\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Status: measured.} Report~01 traced the high-\(N\) Phase~2 failure to a
``runt'' cell manufactured during Phase~1 relaxation, but its measurements were taken
under an energy whose double well was later found to be \emph{mis-discretized}
(coded as \(u^2(1-u)^2\) rather than \(q=u(1-u)\), making the well \(\sim\!25\times\)
too weak; \codefile{docs/reference/phase1\_energy\_discretization\_bug.md}). This
report re-runs the high-\(N\) case on the \emph{corrected} energy. Three findings.
\textbf{(1)} Correcting the well \emph{alone} does not cure the runt: at the original
\(\lambda{=}2.1\) the \(N{=}100\) worst-cell area error is \(21.8\%\), essentially
unchanged from the buggy \(22.5\%\) --- because the mis-scaled well had also made
\(\lambda\) an inert lever. \textbf{(2)} The correction \emph{restores} \(\lambda\) as
an effective knob: a moderate \(\lambda{=}5.1\) drives the worst cell to \(0.8\%\)
(zero cells beyond the \(5\%\) gate), after which Phase~2 refinement \emph{decreases}
perimeter (\(214.34\!\to\!185.25\), \(-13.6\%\)) and stays feasible (equal-area
violation \(\leq 3\times10^{-6}\)) --- where the buggy energy made perimeter rise
(\(217\!\to\!229\)) and crash on infeasibility. \textbf{(3)} The steeper corrected
well introduces a new requirement: at \(N{=}30\), random initialization now
\emph{traps} in the symmetric diffuse state (\(43\%\) worst cell, \(23\) imbalanced
cells) while seeded initialization stays clean (\(0.7\%\)). The operating point is
\emph{corrected energy \(+\) seeded init \(+\) moderate \(\lambda\)}.
\end{abstract}

% =====================================================================
\section{Question}
% =====================================================================
Report~01 (\codefile{01-winner-take-all-partition-gap/}) showed that on the torus at
\(N{=}100\), Phase~1 satisfies the \emph{continuous} equal-area constraint yet the
\emph{discrete} winner-take-all partition contains one grossly undersized ``runt''
cell, which makes Phase~2 perimeter refinement infeasible (perimeter rises, then
``local infeasibility''). Every measurement there was taken with the Phase~1 energy's
double-well term coded as \(\varepsilon^{-1}(u^2(1-u)^2)^{\top}M(u^2(1-u)^2)\). That
term was subsequently found to be a mis-transcription of the paper: the intended
discretization is \(\varepsilon^{-1}q^{\top}Mq\) with \(q=u(1-u)\), so the coded well
was \(\int u^4(1-u)^4\) --- eighth-degree and \(\sim\!25\times\) too weak --- with a
gradient inconsistent with it (full audit:
\codefile{docs/reference/phase1\_energy\_discretization\_bug.md}; derivation of the
corrected form: \codefile{docs/math/06-phase1-energy-discretization/}).

The question this report answers: \emph{on the corrected energy, does the runt
resolve, does Phase~2 then converge, and what re-tuning does the \(\sim\!25\times\)
scale shift require?}

% =====================================================================
\section{Method}
% =====================================================================
All runs share the torus \(T(1.0,0.6)\), seed \(84172851\), and the corrected energy
(except the report-01 buggy anchor, kept for contrast). Two families:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{N=100 runt sweep.} Three runs: the buggy \(\lambda{=}2.1\) anchor from
  report~01; the corrected energy at the \emph{same} \(\lambda{=}2.1\) (isolating the
  well fix); and the corrected energy at \(\lambda{=}5.1\) (the production run).
  For each, the discrete territory of the final \code{solution/} is the winner-take-all
  lumped-mass area \(a_k=\sum_{i:\,\arg\max_{k'} u_{k'}(x_i)=k} v_i\) (\(v\) the lumped
  \(P_1\) mass); we report the worst-cell error \(\max_k|a_k-\bar A|/\bar A\) and the
  full sorted per-cell distribution.
\item \textbf{N=30 init head-to-head.} Two corrected-energy runs identical but for
  \code{init\_method} (\code{random} vs \code{seeded}), \(\lambda{=}2.1\), isolating
  the initialization as the only variable.
\end{itemize}

For the production run we additionally read the Phase~2 campaign's \code{iteration\_*.h5}:
the \code{final\_perimeter} attribute (the per-iteration perimeter) and the
\code{constraint\_violations} array in \code{optimization\_info} (the per-iteration
equal-area feasibility), prepending the extracted Phase~1 perimeter \(214.3413\) as
iteration~0.

\emph{Anchors.} The production worst-cell reconstruction gives \(0.0078\), matching
the run's \code{metadata.yaml} \code{area\_imbalance.worst\_rel\_dev}; the Phase~2
final perimeter \(185.2546\) matches \code{iteration\_020}. These confirm the mesh
rebuild and reshape before any conclusion is drawn.

\section{Results}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{The runt resolves --- but only with the energy fix \emph{and} a moderate \texorpdfstring{\(\lambda\)}{lambda}}
\label{sec:runt}
Table~\ref{tab:runt} and Fig.~\ref{fig:runt} give the central result. Correcting the
well at the original \(\lambda{=}2.1\) barely moves the runt (\(22.5\%\to21.8\%\)):
the fix alone is \emph{not} sufficient. The reason is that the \(\sim\!25\times\)
mis-scaling had also swamped the crispness penalty, leaving \(\lambda\) unable to act
(report~01 swept \(\lambda\in\{1\ldots10\}\) with no effect). On the corrected well
\(\lambda\) is restored as a lever, and a moderate \(\lambda{=}5.1\) equalizes all
\(100\) cells --- worst cell \(0.8\%\), zero cells past the \(5\%\) gate.

\subsection{Phase~2 now decreases perimeter and stays feasible}
\label{sec:phase2}
On the production partition, Phase~2 behaves as intended (Fig.~\ref{fig:phase2}): the
total perimeter falls from the extracted \(214.34\) to \(185.25\) (\(-13.6\%\)) and the
equal-area constraint holds throughout (max violation \(\leq 3\times10^{-6}\); \(4\times
10^{-10}\) at iteration~1). This is the exact opposite of the buggy runs, where the
iteration-0 area violation was \(0.05\)--\(0.16\) and never resolved, so the
feasibility-first solver lengthened perimeter (\(217\to229\)) chasing an unreachable
feasible point. The per-iteration sawtooth in perimeter is the migration subsystem
re-inserting variable points between solves; each solve drives the perimeter back down,
and the best iterate (iteration~20) is the exported production partition.

\subsection{The corrected well makes seeded initialization mandatory}
\label{sec:trap}
The \(\sim\!25\times\)-steeper well has a side effect (Fig.~\ref{fig:trap}): the
\emph{symmetric diffuse state} (every cell at density \(1/N\) everywhere) becomes a
genuine local minimum, where the projected gradient damps perturbations rather than
amplifying them. \textbf{Random} initialization sits essentially at that state and
freezes: at \(N{=}30\), \(\lambda{=}2.1\), it ends with \(23\) cells past the \(5\%\)
gate and a \(43\%\) worst cell. \textbf{Seeded} (farthest-point Voronoi) init, which
hands every cell a contiguous region from iteration~0, ends clean (\(0.7\%\), zero
imbalanced) from the identical config. Under the old flatter well this trap was weak,
so random ``worked'' at low \(N\); on the corrected energy it does not. Random init can
still escape if \(\lambda\) is pushed hard (a level-0 sweep found escape at
\(\lambda\gtrsim10\)), but large \(\lambda\) over-crisps and costs perimeter (\(N{=}30\)
full run at \(\lambda{=}50\): perimeter \(174.2\) vs \(117.8\) for seeded-\(\lambda{=}2.1\),
\(+48\%\)). Hence: \code{init\_method: seeded} \(+\) moderate \(\lambda\).

\section{Conclusion}
% =====================================================================
The high-\(N\) runt of report~01 was, in large part, an artifact of a mis-discretized
energy rather than a fundamental limit at \(N{=}100\). Two consequences of the
\(\sim\!25\times\)-too-weak well drove it: the diffuse ``halo'' a cell parks its mass in
was priced far too cheaply, and the crispness weight \(\lambda\) was rendered inert.
Correcting the well (\codefile{docs/reference/phase1\_energy\_discretization\_bug.md})
removes both. With the corrected energy, seeded initialization, and a \emph{moderate}
\(\lambda{=}5.1\), the \(N{=}100\) torus partition is uniformly equal (worst cell
\(0.8\%\)) and Phase~2 refines it cleanly (perimeter \(-13.6\%\), feasible throughout),
yielding the exported production partition. Neither the energy fix alone nor \(\lambda\)
alone suffices --- they are complementary, and seeded init is now required to avoid the
corrected energy's symmetric trap. No hard discrete-territory or crispness
\emph{constraint} (the front-runner in report~01's ranking) was needed: the soft
\(\lambda\) penalty is enough once the well is priced correctly. The standing
explanation, with the residual near-convergence migration churn noted, is in
\codefile{docs/reference/winner\_take\_all\_partition\_gap.md}~\S8.
